<p><p>The small cell backhaul market comprises various technologies designed to connect small cell networks to the core network. Copper offers cost-effective solutions but has limited bandwidth and distance. Fiber provides high capacity and low latency for future-proofing but can be more expensive and complex to install. Millimeter wave and microwave provide high-speed wireless connections, with millimeter wave offering greater capacity and shorter range. Sub-6 GHz balances coverage and capacity, while satellite backhaul extends connectivity to remote areas, ensuring broader network access.</p></p>
