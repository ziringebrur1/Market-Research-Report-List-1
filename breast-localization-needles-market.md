<p><p>The breast localization needles market primarily includes fine-needle aspiration biopsy (FNAB) and core-needle biopsy (CNB). FNAB utilizes thin needles to extract cellular material, enabling quick diagnosis with minimal trauma. In contrast, CNB employs larger, hollow needles to obtain tissue samples, providing more comprehensive information for pathological assessment. Both techniques play crucial roles in identifying breast abnormalities and guiding treatment decisions, catering to varied clinical needs while ensuring patient comfort and accuracy in diagnosis.</p></p>
